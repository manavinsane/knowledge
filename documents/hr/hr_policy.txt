HR Policy

Access level: HR
Visible to: HR, ADMIN

This document is restricted to HR users and ADMIN users. Employees should not
receive answers from this document unless they also have the HR or ADMIN role.
It contains recruiting workflows, compensation review guidance, employee record
procedures, employee relations escalation notes, and HR-only audit practices.

The internal recruitment tracker code is HR-HIRE-240. The HR document retention
code for employee records is HR-RET-07. The sensitive employee relations
escalation keyword is HR-CASE-SAFE.

Page 1: Recruiting Workflow

The HR candidate pipeline has four stages: applied, screening, panel, offer.
Candidates enter the applied stage when their application is received. HR moves
candidates to screening after checking basic eligibility, role fit, location
requirements, and notice period. Panel interviews are scheduled only after the
screening stage is complete.

Interview feedback must be submitted within 24 hours after the interview. HR
uses this rule to reduce delays and keep hiring decisions consistent. Late
feedback is escalated to the recruiter and the hiring manager. Offer approvals
require HR Lead approval before Finance approval.

Recruiting test facts:
- Internal recruitment tracker code: HR-HIRE-240.
- Pipeline stages: applied, screening, panel, offer.
- Interview feedback deadline: 24 hours after interview.
- Offer approval order: HR Lead first, Finance second.
- Recruiter escalation owner: assigned recruiter.

Page 2: Compensation Review

Compensation calibration happens twice per year, in April and October. HR uses
the internal compensation band reference named HR-COMP-BANDS. Salary exception
requests must include manager justification and HR Director approval.

Employees can see their own payslip through approved self-service tools, but
they cannot access compensation calibration notes. Compensation calibration
notes may include peer comparison, range placement, performance context, and
budget constraints. These notes are restricted to HR and ADMIN users.

Compensation review checklist:
- Confirm employee role and level.
- Compare current compensation with HR-COMP-BANDS.
- Review manager justification.
- Review performance context.
- Check exception history.
- Route salary exception to HR Director if needed.

Compensation test facts:
- Calibration months: April and October.
- Compensation band reference: HR-COMP-BANDS.
- Salary exceptions require HR Director approval.
- Employees cannot access calibration notes.

Page 3: Employee Records

HR record changes require a ticket in the People Operations queue. Legal name
changes require government ID verification. Emergency contact updates are
processed within two business days. The HR document retention code for employee
records is HR-RET-07.

Employee records may include employment status, department, manager, emergency
contact, legal name, work location, and HR case references. HR must verify
identity before processing sensitive changes. HR should never share another
employee's record with an unauthorized employee.

Record update process:
- Receive People Operations ticket.
- Verify requestor identity.
- Check required evidence.
- Update the HR system.
- Add a short audit note.
- Close the ticket with non-sensitive confirmation.

Employee record test facts:
- Record retention code: HR-RET-07.
- Emergency contact update SLA: two business days.
- Legal name changes require government ID verification.
- HR record changes require a People Operations ticket.

Page 4: Employee Relations Escalation

Sensitive employee relations cases use the escalation keyword HR-CASE-SAFE.
Only HR and ADMIN users may access internal investigation notes. The HR
escalation owner is the Employee Relations Manager.

Employee relations cases may include workplace conduct concerns, harassment
complaints, conflict mediation, policy violations, or sensitive manager
escalations. HR must maintain confidentiality, collect factual evidence, avoid
unnecessary disclosure, and document decisions carefully.

Escalation workflow:
- Log the case with HR-CASE-SAFE.
- Assign an Employee Relations Manager.
- Identify immediate risk.
- Preserve relevant evidence.
- Schedule interviews when needed.
- Record findings in restricted HR notes.
- Close the case with documented outcome.

Employee relations test facts:
- Escalation keyword: HR-CASE-SAFE.
- Escalation owner: Employee Relations Manager.
- Investigation notes are restricted to HR and ADMIN.
- HR must preserve relevant evidence.

Page 5: HR Audits and Compliance

HR audits are used to confirm that restricted HR processes are followed
consistently. Audit checks include recruitment stage history, feedback
timeliness, compensation exception approvals, record update evidence, and case
closure documentation.

HR-only audit reminders:
- Do not export employee records to public drives.
- Do not share compensation calibration notes with employees.
- Do not include restricted investigation details in public documents.
- Confirm that HR-HIRE-240 entries have complete stage history.
- Confirm that HR-RET-07 retention handling is followed.

HR audit test facts:
- Recruitment tracker audit code: HR-HIRE-240.
- Employee record retention code: HR-RET-07.
- Compensation band reference: HR-COMP-BANDS.
- Employee relations keyword: HR-CASE-SAFE.
